Marathon Performance Data Analysis


This project uses R to focus on exploring historical race data and building a predictive model for the 2024 Flying Monkey Marathon. By analyzing trends and patterns in runner performance, this project aims to provide insights into individual performance factors and generate a reliable race time prediction.

Executive Summary

Thegoal for this assignment was to predict the inish time for the 2024 Flying Monkey Marathon based on previous race data. Using a dataset from previous marathons, I created a regression model to predict race time based on runner characteristics such as pace and placement. The model showed a signi icant relationship between these variables and inish times, which was validated through residuals analysis and prediction accuracy. The inal prediction time for the upcoming race was approximately 5hrs 20mins 35secs or 5:20:35. This prediction can assist in better understanding runner performance trends and helping participants set realistic race goals.

Executive Summary

In this analysis, I began exploring the two datasets (MONKEY and RUNLOG) to understand their structure and to ind any potential issues with missing or inconsistent data. Missing values were identi ied, and these issues were ixed through data cleaning procedures, including replacing missing values for time-related variables and converting date columns into the appropriate date format.

I then began visualizing various relationships in the data. For the RUNLOG dataset, histograms and boxplots were used to assess the distribution of mileage, total time, and weather temperature, as well as how these factors differ by pace type. Scatter plots were used to explore relationships between mileage, total time, and weather temperature, while time series plots helped analyze trends in mileage and race time over different race dates. These visualizations helped identify key patterns and outliers in the data.

For the MONKEY dataset, similar visualizations were created, where I focused on total time across race dates and the distribution of average pace. These provided insights into how performance varied over time and among different racing dates.

A linear regression model was built using the MONKEY dataset to predict total time (Total_Time_S) based on several predictors, including pace, race placement, and age placement factors. The regression analysis provided estimates of how these variables inluence race time. The model’s residuals were examined to assess its it and ensure the predictions were reliable.

Finally, the model was used to predict the race time for an upcoming event, based on average values for pace, race placement, and age. The prediction was converted into hours, minutes, and seconds to provide a clear estimate of the expected race completion time. This model serves as a useful tool for predicting future race outcomes based on past data.

In comparing the predicted marathon time of 5:20:35, based on a pace of 12.19784 minutes per mile from the most recent Flying Monkey Marathon, to the actual time of 5:38:42 from the 2024 Chickamauga Marathon, there is a difference of 18 minutes and 7 seconds. The predicted time was calculated from the runner’s previous pace, placement, and age placement, which assumes consistent conditions across marathons. However, the actual time is longer, likely inluenced by external factors such as race conditions, weather, fatigue, or course dif iculty, which are not accounted for in the pace-based estimation. This suggests variations in race-day conditions can lead to differences in overall performance.
